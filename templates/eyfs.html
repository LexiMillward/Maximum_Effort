The Early Years Foundation Stage

I am required by law to teach the new & revised Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) to all the children in my care and supervision from birth to the September after their 5th birthday. These classes are a very important stage and time in your childs life as it helps your child become more confident and be school ready as well as preparing them for future structure, learning & success. From when your child is born up until the age of 5, their early years experience should be supported but made happy, exciting, fun. This will directly support their development, care and learning needs.


What is the EYFS Framework?

The EYFS Framework was created & exists to support all professionals working in this industry and many more with children involved between birth and the age of 5 to provide structure and help your child develop and grow. It was developed by a number of early years experts and parents collaborating together to agree and decide on what is needed when suporting and developing children at this stage in their lives. The Framework is constantly updating & regularly revised, with more focus on the things that matter most. The Framwork also has major emphasis on keeping children safe and also on your own role in helping your child develop.

The EYFS is based on 4 important principles;

  • A Unique Child:
    • Each child is a competent & fast learner from birth who can be resilient, capable, confident and self-assured.
  • Positive Relationships:
    • Children will learn & grow to be strong and independent from a base of loving consistent relationships with their parents and/or a key person.
  • Environments:
    • The environment must & will play a key role in supporting and extending children's development and learning in each and every way possible.
  • Learning and Development:
    • Children are very unique & develop & learn in many different ways. They also develop at different rates. All areas of learning and evelopment are treated equally as important and are all connected.


The Areas of Learning and Development

The EYFS has 7 areas of learning and development.

There are 3 prime areas in which we encourage children develop first, these are;
- Personal, Social & Emotional Development;
- Communication & language;
- Physical Development.

These prime areas are the most essential for your child's healthy development and future learning. Its is believed that as children grow, the prime areas will help them develop skills in 4 specific areas, these are;
- Literacy;
- Mathematics;
- Understanding the world;
- Expressive Arts and Design.
These 7 development areas are used to plan for your child's unique needs and develop their interests. this is done in both indoor and outdoor environments
