Questions &

Answers

Will there be a time to discuss my child's development with the childminder?


Although parents will be in contact with myself every day there may be a time when we will need to discuss your child's development while they are not present. I am more than happy to do this when it is convenient for both parties. I will also share your child's learning journey and progress and next steps with you on a regular basis throughout the year.


Lunch time - What if my child is a fussy eater


Children are to bring packed lunch, and no nut based products. You may find that because your child is in new surroundings and is with other children they may change habits I will encourage but not force your child to eat food that they genuinely do not like. Children tend to eat when they feel hungry so don't worry if I tell you they havent eaten very much on a particular day this is just for your information. Food consumed will be recorded daily in your child's daily diary.


What happens if I am late collecting my child from the childminder?


I understand that with busy working lives and traffic people do get held up. Don't worry about your child they will be safe and well looked after until you arrive. I may have to make a charge if you are regularly later than the agreed collection time.


What if my child becomes ill or has an accident whilst with the childminder?


Please bring any medications or inhalers with clear written names and directions of use as per guidance from the childs GP. Minor accidents or injuries will be treated with first aid and will be recorded which will be shown and signed by you. For more serious injuries every effort will be made to contact you and professional medical advice will be sort. If your child gets ill then every effort will be made to contact you or someone else on the emergency contact list to collect the child.


What does the childminder expect you to provide?


For children of a younger age I ask that you provide all consumables necessary for changing nappies. For slightly older children a change of clothes, pants, trousers and socks would be required for those little accidents. Sun cream, hats will be required for sunny days and warm clothes for colder days.
